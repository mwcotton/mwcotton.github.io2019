Featured Projects

A small selection of things that I have enjoyed working on. Please contact me if you would like more information about any of these projects.

Searching for Hydrogen Exospheres

A two term project searching for hydrogen exospheres around the TRAPPIST-1 planets, supervised by Professor Julien de Wit.

We developed new processes to handle raw astronomical data that integrated with existing pipeines. Our major innovation was to use Markov chain Monte Carlo techniques to fit more complex models, allowing for higher signal to noise ratio from faint stars.

Model of the spectrum observed from Earth, accounting for a range of astronomical effects.

Networks: Along the Right Lines

During a course modelling environmental complexity, I undertook an individual project investigating the structure of the London Underground Network.

I was interested in the substructures on the network (the different lines) and explored their proteries using random walkers, average distances and return times. The properties on the lines fell into two groups and I proposed a framework to explain these obsrevations.

Investigating the diffusion of random walkers on the network and the relation to the lines (the Circle line is shown).

Population Dynamics

A short project, working with Robert King, to investigate the Lotka-Volterra predator prey system from stochastically modelling interactions between individuals.

We furthered this model to a spatially extended system which could be used to investigate the spread of diseases or the effectiveness of sampling techniques. The visualisation tools we produced aided understanding of the the system, and the effects of initial population size, system size etc.

Tool to visualise the populations and spatial dependence of a predator prey system.
